## How Can I Prevent Chafing During Ultra Runs?

Chafing during ultra runs is a common issue, but it's manageable with the right strategies. Use anti-chafe products such as BodyGlide or Squirrel’s Nut Butter on potential hotspots, like thighs, underarms, and nipples. Opt for clothing made from moisture-wicking and breathable fabrics to minimize friction. Additionally, ensuring your gear, like hydration packs, fits properly can prevent unwanted rubbing.

## What Clothing Helps Prevent Chafing?

Selecting the right clothing for ultra running can significantly reduce the risk of chafing. Look for seamless options and moisture-wicking materials such as polyester blends. Brands like Smartwool and Under Armour offer excellent lightweight and breathable options. Compression shorts can also help by reducing movement and friction between the thighs. Always test your gear during [training for your first 50K](/training/first-50k) to ensure a good fit and comfort.

## Are There Special Socks for Chafing Prevention?

Yes, special socks can aid in chafe prevention during ultra runs. Look for socks made with merino wool or synthetic blends for moisture management and breathability. Brands like Injinji and Balega offer toe socks and seamless options that help reduce friction and blistering, which is crucial over ultra-distances.

## How Often Should I Reapply Anti-Chafe Products?

The frequency of reapplying anti-chafe products depends on conditions and personal needs. For most ultra runners, applying once at the start is often sufficient. However, during particularly long runs or in hot and humid conditions, reapplying every few hours can be beneficial. Carrying a small stick of BodyGlide or Squirrel’s Nut Butter in your pack for mid-race touch-ups can be a game-changer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

How does chafing affect ultra running performance?  
Chafing can severely impact performance due to increased discomfort and potential for infection. It can cause runners to slow down or even stop, turning a simple annoyance into a race-ending problem.

Can I make my own anti-chafe salve?  
Yes, some runners create homemade salves using ingredients like coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter. While these can be effective, commercial products like BodyGlide and Squirrel’s Nut Butter often provide more consistent results.

Is powder effective for chafe prevention?  
Powder can help in moisture absorption and reducing friction, but it often needs reapplication more frequently than balms during long runs. It's useful as a supplementary aid alongside other anti-chafe products.

What if running in the rain? Does it change chafe prevention strategies?  
Running in the rain increases the risk of chafing due to wet clothing sticking and rubbing against the skin. Waterproof or water-resistant anti-chafe products should be applied liberally, and quick-drying clothing is beneficial.

Does body weight affect chafing in ultra running?  
Body weight can influence chafing since areas with more skin folds or larger surface areas may experience increased friction. Proper fitting gear and strategic use of anti-chafe products can help mitigate this risk.
