Quick Answer: Whether you're a seasoned athlete or preparing for your first ultra, ultra running podcasts offer a wealth of knowledge and inspiration. The best trail running podcasts cover topics like training tips, gear recommendations, and personal stories from ultra runners. Shows such as "Trail Runner Nation," "The Ultra Running Podcast," and "Ginger Runner Live" provide both entertainment and valuable insights for your running journey.

## What Are the Best Ultra Running Podcasts?

If you’re stepping into the world of ultras or a veteran looking to stay inspired, tuning into the right ultra running podcasts can transform your running experience. Here are some standout shows you shouldn’t miss:

### Trail Runner Nation

This podcast is a favorite among both beginner and seasoned runners. Hosted by Don Freeman, Scott Warr, and Faith Goss, "Trail Runner Nation" explores everything from gear and nutrition to mental strategies. Their episodes featuring interviews with elite runners and industry experts provide a deep dive into the nuances of ultra running.

[Garmin Forerunner 255](/gear)

### The Ultra Running Podcast

Hosted by Eric Schranz, this podcast focuses on stories and journeys from ultra runners of all levels. Eric’s engaging interviews with professional runners, as well as everyday athletes, make it one of the best trail running podcasts for those who love hearing about personal challenges and triumphs in the ultra world.

### Ginger Runner Live

Ethan Newberry, known as The Ginger Runner, covers a wide range of topics in his podcast, from race reviews to in-depth interviews with notable figures in the ultra community. His energetic and motivational style is perfect for keeping you entertained during those long training runs.

[Hoka Speedgoat 6 on Amazon](https://amzn.to/41b2CQt)

### Ten Junk Miles

Running isn't just about the grind; it’s also about the community and stories we share. "Ten Junk Miles" combines humor and running insight, hosted by a diverse panel of ultra-runners. Each episode captures the camaraderie of the ultra community and is perfect for a light-hearted listening experience.

[Nathan Pinnacle 4L on Amazon](https://amzn.to/4bObB06)

### Running Virtually

"Running Virtually" focuses on making ultra distances more approachable for beginners through practical advice and community stories. This podcast emphasizes the importance of mindset and preparation, making it essential for new ultra runners seeking confidence in their journey.

## How Can Podcasts Enhance My Ultra Running Experience?

Listening to ultra running podcasts is like having a personal coach and cheerleading squad in your earbuds. These shows can:

- Provide expert advice on [training for your first 50K](/training/first-50k), saving you from common pitfalls.
- Offer gear recommendations, from shoes to [hydration packs](/gear/packs), to keep you well-prepared for any trail.
- Inspire you with real-life stories that put your own challenges into perspective.
- Help you discover new trails or races that might fit your running goals.

## What Should I Consider When Choosing A Podcast?

When selecting the best trail running podcasts, consider:

1. Hosts' Expertise: Look for hosts with backgrounds in ultra running for credible advice.
2. Content Variety: Choose shows that cover a mix of technical advice and personal stories.
3. Episode Length: Align with your available listening time, from short insights to longer, in-depth interviews.
4. Community Engagement: Some podcasts offer listener interactions, which can be excellent for advice and support.

## How Often Should I Listen to Podcasts During Training?

The frequency of listening to ultra running shows varies with personal preference and training schedules. However, here’s a general guide:

- Long Runs: Great for longer podcasts that dig deep into stories and advice.
- Recovery Sessions: Ideal for lighter shows with humor or motivational content.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are running podcasts motivational for first-timers?

Absolutely, tuning into running podcasts can be incredibly motivating for first-timers. They offer not only expert insights but also energizing stories from runners who’ve been through similar challenges, helping you stay committed and inspired.

### How do I fit podcast listening into my routine?

Integrate listening during your warm-ups, long runs, or cool-downs. Keeping your playlist ready ensures you can easily transition from one activity to another without skipping a beat in your training schedule.

### Do podcasts help with race day strategies?

Yes, many podcasts feature episodes dedicated to race day strategies, providing tips on pacing, nutrition, and mental preparation. These episodes can offer you a comprehensive understanding of what to expect and how to plan your race day approach.

### Can a podcast replace other training methods?

While podcasts provide valuable information and motivation, they should complement other training resources such as physical training plans, books, and in-person coaching. Consider them a supplementary tool to enhance your overall training experience.

### What if I don’t have time to listen to long episodes?

Select podcasts offer bite-sized episodes or summaries that deliver essential insights quickly. You can also listen at increased playback speeds or during commutes and household chores, making the most of every moment available.

### What’s the best time of day to listen to podcasts?

There’s no one-size-fits-all answer; it depends on personal schedules and preferences. Morning listeners might find them energizing for starting the day, while evening listeners can enjoy them as a relaxing wind-down after a day of training or work.
