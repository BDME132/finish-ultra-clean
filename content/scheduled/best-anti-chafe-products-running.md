Every ultra runner has a chafing story. The best ones are from races. The worst ones are from not knowing these products existed. Here's the definitive ranking.

## 1. Body Glide Original — Best All-Around ($9–$12)

Body Glide is the default anti-chafe recommendation for runners worldwide, and with good reason. It goes on dry (like a deodorant stick), creates an invisible barrier that reduces friction, and doesn't stain clothing. The original formula is appropriate for thighs, underarms, nipples, neck, and any vest contact point.

**Best for:** All-purpose daily and race use, dry to moderate conditions
**Lasts:** 3–6 hours depending on sweat rate and conditions
**Available in:** Regular (1.5oz) and travel size (0.45oz — fits in vest pocket)

[Shop Body Glide Original on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Body+Glide+Original&tag=finishultra-20)

## 2. Squirrel's Nut Butter — Best for Wet Conditions and Long Races ($14–$18)

Squirrel's Nut Butter uses a petroleum and wax base that is significantly more water-resistant than Body Glide. It stays on through rain, stream crossings, and heavy sweating better than any other mainstream product. The trade-off: it's greasier to apply and can stain light-colored clothing.

The ultra running community considers Squirrel's the serious long-race option — what you use when Body Glide isn't enough.

**Best for:** Wet races, 8+ hour efforts, runners who sweat heavily
**Lasts:** 5–8 hours in wet conditions
**Available in:** Small tin and larger jar

[Shop Squirrel's Nut Butter on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Squirrels+Nut+Butter&tag=finishultra-20)

## 3. Trail Toes Ultra-Distance Foot Cream — Best for Feet ($20)

Trail Toes is specifically formulated for feet and toes on long trail runs. It's thicker than Body Glide, provides protection against blisters as well as chafing, and the formula holds up through wet trail conditions that compromise other products.

Apply to the entire foot and between every toe before every long run. Runners who switch to Trail Toes from regular petroleum jelly typically report significant blister reduction within 2–3 long runs.

**Best for:** Foot and toe application, wet terrain, blister-prone runners
**Use alongside:** Body Glide or Squirrel's for body application

[Shop Trail Toes on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Trail+Toes+Ultra&tag=finishultra-20)

## 4. Body Glide For Her — Best Formulation for Sensitive Skin ($9–$12)

Functionally similar to Body Glide Original but formulated with aloe vera and plant-derived ingredients that are less irritating for runners with sensitive skin. The applicator is slightly smaller than the original. Many runners (not exclusively women) prefer the formula for neck and underarm application where skin can be more sensitive.

[Shop Body Glide For Her on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Body+Glide+For+Her&tag=finishultra-20)

## 5. NipGuard Nipple Protectors — Best for Nipple Chafing (Men) ($15)

Adhesive fabric patches that stick directly over nipples and remain in place through sweat and rain. More reliable than balm for nipple protection on runs over 4 hours. Each patch lasts one run. A pack of 60 patches covers an entire training season.

[Shop NipGuard on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=NipGuard+nipple+protectors&tag=finishultra-20)

## The Application Strategy That Actually Works

Apply anti-chafe in this order before any run over 90 minutes:

1. Trail Toes to entire foot and between every toe
2. Body Glide to inner thighs (several passes)
3. Body Glide or Squirrel's to underarms
4. Body Glide to neck, shoulders, and any vest contact points
5. NipGuard patches or Body Glide over nipples (men)

Reapply Body Glide to thighs and underarms at any aid station where you have drop bag access. A travel-size Body Glide in your vest costs nothing in weight and can save your race.
