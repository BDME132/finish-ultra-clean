Quick Answer: To avoid going out too fast in an ultramarathon, start at a comfortable pace that's slower than your goal race pace, typically around 60-70% effort. Focus on staying patient during the first 10-15 kilometers and resist the urge to speed up. Use a GPS watch to monitor your pace and establish a conservative strategy, allowing you to conserve energy for later stages of the race.  

## What Strategies Help Control Your Pace at the Start?

Keeping your cool at the start of an ultramarathon is crucial. By controlling your pace, you set the stage for a stronger finish. One effective strategy is to set a [pace plan](/tools/pace-calculator) prior to race day. Calculate your expected finish time, then determine a starting pace that feels sustainable, typically 20-30 seconds slower per kilometer than your target race pace. A GPS watch is invaluable here — it helps ensure you don’t get caught up in the excitement of the start.

### Use a GPS Watch

GPS watches, like the [Garmin Forerunner 255](Kapex), can help you keep track of your pace and remind you to stick to your plan. Set your watch to alert you if you exceed your intended starting pace. These alerts can keep you grounded and focused during the adrenaline rush of the opening miles.

### Practice Consistent Training Runs

Incorporate training runs that focus on pace consistency. Practice running the first part of your long runs slower than the later segments. This will reinforce the discipline needed on race day. During these runs, pay attention to your breathing and effort levels. 

## Why Do New Runners Go Out Too Fast?

It's common for new ultrarunners to go out too fast because excitement and adrenaline can mask effort levels. The euphoria of starting, the scenery, and the crowd can be misleading, making your usual pace seem easier. However, this can lead to early fatigue and a challenging later race.

### Overcoming the Initial Adrenaline Rush

Mindful breathing and a focus on your pace can help overcome the initial rush. A solid ultra running [strategy](/training/first-50k) includes anticipating this excitement and having tools like mantras or music to keep your mind centered.

## How Do I Set an Effective Race Start Strategy?

Establishing an effective race start strategy involves planning and mental preparation. Before the race, review the course profile and conditions. Adjust your starting pace based on elevation changes, weather, and trail conditions. Know the areas where you need to hold back and where you can push a bit more.

### Consider the First Few Miles as a Warm-Up

View the first miles as a warm-up to acclimate your body and mind to the ultra running experience. This mindset helps in preventing burnout and allows a more strategic use of energy reserves.

## What Mental Techniques Can Stop You from Going Out Too Fast?

Mental preparation is as vital as physical training in ultras. Techniques like mindfulness and visualizations can help maintain a steady pace. Before race day, practice visualizing the start of the race and how you will consciously control your excitement and nerves.

### Use Visualization and Mantras

Visualize your start line experience and repeat calming mantras. Phrases like "steady and strong" or "patience now, power later" can keep you focused on the bigger picture rather than the initial buzz.

## When Should You Speed Up in a Race?

The ideal time to pick up pace in a race is beyond the halfway mark. Use the earlier part of the race to conserve energy. If feeling strong after you've crossed the 25 km mark, consider gradually increasing your speed. This allows you to take advantage of any energy inventory you've preserved by not going out too fast in the ultramarathon’s early stages.

### Monitor Your Energy Levels Throughout

Constantly assess your physical and mental energy. Increase pace only when you're confident in your stamina and the remaining distance. Remember, at no point should you be sprinting; ultras demand strategic energy conservation.

## How Can Gear and Nutrition Influence Your Start?

Gear and nutrition play significant roles in your start pace. Wear tested [shoes](/gear/shoes) and comfortable ultramarathon gear like the [Hoka Speedgoat 6](AFFILIATE) to prevent distractions from discomfort or blisters during the race onset. 

### Pre-Race Nutrition and Hydration

Ensure your pre-race meals are easily digestible with a balanced mix of carbs and proteins, and hydrate well. Poor nutrition can lead to unnecessary energy expenditure if your body isn't well-fueled at the outset.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Why is it a mistake to go out too fast in an ultramarathon?  
Racing too fast at the start depletes your energy reserves early. This can cause severe fatigue, leading to a slower finish or not finishing at all. Starting conservatively increases the probability of maintaining a consistent pace throughout.

How can a pacer help during an ultra?  
A pacer can guide you to maintain a steady pace, especially during difficult segments. They remind you of your strategy and provide motivation when fatigue sets in, helping you avoid early burnout caused by starting too fast.

What are the benefits of using a heart rate monitor?  
Heart rate monitors offer real-time feedback on your exertion levels. This can help ensure your pace corresponds with your strategy and effort goals, securing you from the trap of going out too fast.

How can I recover if I've already gone out too fast?  
If you start too fast, slow down as soon as you recognize it. Refocus on hydration and energy intake, and modulate your speed to recover gradually. Give yourself time to recuperate and avoid further taxing your body.

What's a good sign that my pace is too fast?  
Early signs include rapid breathing, an elevated heart rate, or feeling winded when you're at what should be a comfortable pace. These indicate your pace may be unsustainable for the race's entirety.

Why is it important to adjust pace according to terrain?  
Terrain affects exertion; uphill or technical sections may need you to slow down to prevent exhaustion, while downhill segments can be used to recover. Adjusting ensures energy is properly distributed over the entire course.
