Nutrition is where most first-time ultra runners fail. Not fitness, not mental game — food timing. Here is a specific, actionable hour-by-hour plan for a 7-hour 50K. Adjust timing for your expected finish time.

## The Core Principles Before the Plan

**Start early.** Fueling before you feel hungry prevents the blood sugar crash that takes 30+ minutes to recover from.

**Consistency over volume.** 200 calories every 45 minutes beats 600 calories at mile 15.

**Liquid before solid in the first half.** Your gut is most efficient early in the race. Liquid calories (Tailwind) are processed faster and with less GI stress.

**Real food in the second half.** Your preference for sweet gels will fade around hour 4. Plan for salty, savory options at later aid stations.

## Pre-Race (2–3 hours before start)

**Meal:** 400–600 calories of carbohydrate-rich food you've eaten before training runs. Oatmeal, toast with peanut butter, or a bagel with banana. Keep fat and fiber low.

**Hydration:** 16–20oz water with a small amount of electrolytes. You're starting the race well-hydrated.

**Avoid:** Coffee in excess (mild caffeine is fine), high-fiber foods, dairy, anything new.

## Hour 1 (Miles 0–7, approx.)

**Target:** 150–200 calories, 500mg sodium

**What to do:**
- Start sipping [Tailwind](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Tailwind+Endurance+Fuel&tag=finishultra-20) from flask at 30 minutes in — one serving (100 calories, 300mg sodium) in 24oz water
- Eat nothing solid in the first 45 minutes unless you feel hungry
- First solid food at mile 5–6: at an aid station, grab a banana or pretzels

**Mindset:** You feel good. Your glycogen stores are full. The temptation is to skip eating. Don't — you're investing for hours 4 and 5.

## Hour 2 (Miles 7–14, approx.)

**Target:** 200–250 calories, 500–700mg sodium

**What to do:**
- Continue sipping Tailwind every 20 minutes
- At the mile 8–10 aid station: eat 1–2 real food items (potatoes with salt, PB&J quarter, banana)
- Take a [SaltStick capsule](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=SaltStick+Caps&tag=finishultra-20) if running in heat

**Check-in:** Is your stomach happy? Are you actually eating on schedule? Are your flasks getting emptier at the right rate?

## Hour 3 (Miles 14–20, approx.)

**Target:** 250–300 calories, 600–800mg sodium

**What to do:**
- Increase caloric intake — glycogen depletion is beginning to accelerate
- At mid-race aid station: eat more substantially. A cup of broth if available, real food, gels if they still sound good
- If gels are becoming unappealing, switch entirely to real food from aid stations
- Consider first caffeine dose if you've been saving it: 1 caffeinated gel or 2 caffeine chews

**Watch for:** First signs of stomach distress. If nausea begins, switch to plain water for 20 minutes and eat only bland foods (pretzels, banana) before reintroducing Tailwind.

## Hour 4 (Miles 20–25, approx.)

**Target:** 250–300 calories, 700–900mg sodium

**What to do:**
- Sweet gels may be increasingly unpleasant. Prioritize real food and salty options
- Broth at aid stations is excellent for sodium and warmth
- Flat Coca-Cola provides caffeine + simple sugar + settles nausea for many runners
- Consider second caffeine dose if you have one planned

**Critical window:** Miles 20–25 are where most nutrition failures happen. Runners who under-fueled in hours 1–3 are now experiencing blood sugar drops that manifest as fatigue, mood changes, and slowing pace. Eating consistently through hours 1–3 is the entire solution.

## Hour 5–Finish (Miles 25–31)

**Target:** Whatever your stomach will accept, prioritizing calories and sodium

**What to do:**
- Eat at every aid station, even small amounts
- Flat Coke, broth, potatoes with salt, and any salty real food are your best options
- Continue hydration — dehydration worsens in final miles as gut blood flow reduces further
- Final caffeine dose 30–45 minutes from expected finish if needed

**Mindset:** You're nearly done. Every calorie you eat now is supporting the finish, not the distance. Keep fueling even if it's just a few bites at each station.

## The Quick Reference Card

| Time | Target calories | Key action |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-race | 400–600 | Familiar carb meal, 3 hours before |
| Hour 1 | 150–200 | Start Tailwind at 30 min |
| Hour 2 | 200–250 | Add real food at aid stations |
| Hour 3 | 250–300 | Increase food, consider caffeine |
| Hour 4 | 250–300 | Switch to salty/real food, broth |
| Hour 5+ | Whatever works | Eat at every station, keep moving |

Write this on your arm in waterproof marker before the race. The decisions are hard to remember at mile 22.
