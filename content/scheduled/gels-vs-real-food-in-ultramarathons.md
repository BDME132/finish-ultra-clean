Quick Answer: In ultramarathons, choosing between gels and real food depends on personal preferences and nutritional needs. Gels offer convenience and quick energy, ideal for immediate glycogen replenishment, while real food provides variety and longer-lasting fuel but may be harder to digest on the move. It's crucial to test both methods during training to find what works best for your digestive system and energy needs.

## Are Gels or Real Foods Better for Ultramarathons?

Choosing between gels and real food in an ultramarathon is often debated among runners. Gels, such as Maurten Gel 100 and GU Energy Gel, provide fast-releasing carbohydrates and are easy to consume while moving. However, they can lead to stomach issues if not adequately tested beforehand or if consumed in excess. Real food, like bananas, peanut butter sandwiches, or salty snacks, offers nutritional diversity, providing fats and proteins important for sustained energy. While real food can be more satisfying and easier on the stomach for some, it requires more planning and might slow you down if you need frequent stops.

### Pros and Cons of Energy Gels

- Pros:
  - Quick to consume, minimizing time spent on nutrition during races.
  - High in carbohydrates, ideal for replenishing glycogen quickly.
  - Portable and easy to carry in packs or pockets.
- Cons:
  - Can cause stomach issues if taken too frequently or without proper hydration.
  - Some runners find the taste or texture unappealing after several hours.

### Pros and Cons of Real Food

- Pros:
  - Provides a variety of nutrients, including proteins and fats which can aid endurance.
  - More palatable, often improving morale during long efforts.
  - Can be tailored to personal tastes and needs, from sweet to savory options.
- Cons:
  - Bulkier to carry, requiring strategic packing or drop bags.
  - More challenging to consume while moving, potentially affecting pace.

## How to Balance Gels and Real Food in Your Nutrition Strategy?

An effective ultramarathon nutrition strategy often combines both energy gels and real food. You can use gels for immediate energy boosts, especially on challenging portions of the race, and real food for more substantial, satiating meals during brief rest periods.

### Using Energy Gels Effectively

To incorporate energy gels effectively, consider the following tips:

- Stay Hydrated: Always consume gels with water. This helps prevent gastrointestinal distress and aids the absorption of carbohydrates.
- Schedule Consumption: Plan to consume a gel every 30 to 60 minutes, aligning with your body's caloric needs and your race duration.
- Test in Training: Test different gel brands and flavors in training to ensure they agree with your stomach.

### Incorporating Real Food

Incorporating real food requires strategic planning and testing. Here are some tips:

- Diverse Options: Pack a mix of sweet and savory foods like trail mix, peanut butter wraps, or energy bars.
- Plan Rest Stops: Utilize aid stations or scheduled stops to consume real food calmly.
- Track Performance: Keep notes during training runs to identify which foods provide sustained energy without causing digestion issues.

## How Do GI Issues Affect Your Choice?

Gastrointestinal (GI) issues can significantly impact your ultramarathon by reducing your ability to absorb nutrients effectively and causing physical discomfort. Here’s how gels vs real food choices can influence your GI comfort:

### Avoiding GI Distress

- Gels: Ensure adequate water intake when consuming gels. Some runners find that sugar alcohols or artificial ingredients in gels can be hard on the stomach, so choose natural brands if sensitive.
- Real Food: Opt for low-fiber, easily digestible foods that you've tested in training. Real foods with high-fat content can sometimes cause sluggishness, so balance is key.

### Personal Experience Matters

Your individual experience is a crucial guide. Testing different foods and gels during training will help you discover optimal combinations. Always plan for contingencies, like your preferred foods failing to be appealing or having unexpected effects during the race.

## What Role Does Hydration Play in Ultramarathon Nutrition?

Hydration is the cornerstone of any ultramarathon nutrition strategy, directly influencing how well you can digest and utilize the nutrients from both gels and real food. Proper hydration facilitates nutrient absorption and helps prevent hyponatremia, a dangerous condition caused by electrolyte imbalance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

What are the benefits of using energy gels in ultramarathons?

Energy gels provide a convenient and quick source of carbohydrates during ultramarathons, making them ideal for on-the-go energy replenishment. They are compact, easy to carry, and can significantly reduce the time you spend managing nutrition, allowing you to maintain a steady pace.

Why is real food important in ultra race nutrition strategy?

Real food provides nutritional diversity, including fats and proteins, which can enhance endurance and keep you feeling satisfied. Eating real food can help alleviate the monotony of flavors and textures that comes with relying solely on gels, offering an emotional and physical pick-me-up during long distances.

How can I test my nutrition plan for an ultra race?

Begin testing your nutrition plan during training runs, introducing both gels and real foods to your routine. Pay attention to how different foods affect your energy, mood, and gastrointestinal comfort. Adjust based on these observations to refine your race day plan, ensuring it supports your caloric needs without causing distress.

What should I do if I experience GI issues during a race?

If GI issues arise, slow your pace and focus on hydration, as this can help alleviate symptoms by aiding digestion. Opt for easily digestible, familiar foods, and give your stomach time to settle. Sometimes, taking a short break can help reset your system, allowing you to continue effectively.

How often should I consume energy gels during a 50K?

For a 50K, consuming an energy gel every 45 minutes to an hour is a reasonable starting point. This interval allows you to maintain steady carbohydrate intake while adjusting to your body's specific demand for calories and energy during the race.

What are some good real food options to try during an ultra race?

Consider trying bananas, boiled potatoes, nut butter sandwiches, energy bars, or saltines. These options provide varying macro-nutrients and are generally easy to digest. Always verify that these choices suit your training preferences before race day.
