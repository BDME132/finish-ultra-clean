Quick Answer: The ideal dinner the night before an ultra includes a balance of carbohydrates, moderate protein, and low fat. Consider meals such as pasta with lean protein or rice with vegetables and tofu. Avoid high-fiber and high-fat foods that can cause digestion issues during the race. Hydration is also key, so drink enough fluids to maintain optimal levels without overdoing it.

## What Should You Eat the Night Before an Ultramarathon?

When facing your first 50K, knowing what to eat the night before an ultra is crucial for ensuring your body has the fuel it needs. Carbohydrate-rich meals are essential because they replenish glycogen stores, the primary energy source during long runs. Consider these practical meal ideas:

- Pasta with a lean protein source: Choose whole-grain pasta with grilled chicken or fish, seasoned with a light sauce.
- Rice bowls with vegetables: Brown rice with roasted vegetables and tofu or a small portion of lean beef can satisfy and sustain.
- Sweet potatoes with beans: These complex carbs paired with a protein source can provide long-lasting energy.

Avoid heavy sauces and too much fiber, as they can lead to stomach discomfort during your run.

## How Much Should You Focus on Carb Loading?

Carb loading before an ultramarathon can improve performance by increasing muscle glycogen stores. Start increasing your carbohydrate intake two to three days before the race, focusing on 60-70% of your total calorie intake from carbs. This doesn't mean stuffing yourself with pasta but rather shifting the balance of your meals.

- Choose quality carbs: Whole grains, fruits, and starchy vegetables should be your primary sources. 
- Manage portions: Moderation is key to avoid feeling sluggish or overly full.

Remember, it's essential to practice carb loading in your training to know how your body reacts, as this helps to refine your [pre-race nutrition](/gear/nutrition) plan.

## Which Foods Should You Avoid Before Race Day?

Certain foods can derail your race day performance if consumed in excess the night before:

- High-fat foods: Avoid greasy takeouts or butter-heavy dishes that can impede digestion.
- High-fiber foods: Large salads or fiber-rich beans might cause gastrointestinal distress mid-race.
- Sugary snacks: While tempting, a sugar spike doesn't provide lasting energy and can lead to a crash.

By focusing on balanced, easy-to-digest meals, you'll give your body the best chance to perform well [training for your first 50K](/training/first-50k).

## Why is Hydration Important the Night Before an Ultra?

Drinking enough fluids before an ultramarathon is vital for hydration. Your body loses fluids as you exert yourself, so ensure you're hydrated without overdoing it:

- Electrolyte balance: Consider a light electrolyte drink with dinner.
- Monitor your urine color: It should be a light straw color, indicating optimal hydration.

Remember not to flood your system with water; balance is key.

## What Other Pre-Race Preparations Should You Consider?

A good night's sleep combined with a solid pre-race nutrition strategy can boost your performance come race day. Reduce screen time and caffeine intake the evening before to promote restful sleep and manage anxiety. Also, lay out your gear and set a relaxed morning schedule, leaving ample time for a calming pre-race breakfast.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Can I try new foods the night before an ultra?

No, avoid new foods before a race as they might upset your stomach. Stick to tried-and-tested meals you know will sit well with you.

How much should I eat the night before an ultramarathon?

Consume enough to feel satisfied without feeling overstuffed. A moderate portion rich in carbs, some protein, and low fat works best.

Should I skip dinner to avoid gut issues?

Skipping dinner is not advisable as it may lead to low energy levels. Instead, choose easily digestible foods and avoid irritants.

Is carbo-loading the night before enough for an ultra?

Carbo-loading should happen two to three days before the race, not just the night before. Gradually increase your carbohydrate intake for best results.

Can I drink alcohol the night before the race?

It's best to avoid alcohol as it can dehydrate you and interfere with restful sleep, which is critical for peak performance.

What should be my breakfast strategy on race day?

Eat a light, familiar meal 2-3 hours before the race, such as oatmeal with banana or toast and peanut butter. Hydrate well and allow time for it to settle.
