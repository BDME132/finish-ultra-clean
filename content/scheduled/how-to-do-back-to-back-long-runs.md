## What Are Back to Back Long Runs in Ultra Training?

Back to back long runs are a crucial component in ultra training, where you run long distances two days in a row. This method helps simulate the fatigue you'll experience during an ultra event. By tackling consecutive long runs, you train your body to handle tired legs and learn how to manage fatigue effectively. [Check out this training guide](/training/first-50k) to align your workouts with your goals.

### Why Are Double Long Runs Beneficial?

Double long run training is advantageous as it conditions your muscles and mental fortitude. Running on tired legs is a reality in any ultra event, and this regimen helps you prepare psychologically and physically. Additionally, it enhances stamina and teaches you pacing over extended periods.

## How Do You Start Incorporating Back to Back Long Runs?

Incorporating back to back long runs into your plan should be done gradually. Start with a distance that feels challenging yet manageable on the first day, followed by a slightly shorter run the next day. Initially, aim for a 10-mile run followed by a 6-mile one. As you adapt, increase both distances, keeping your overall weekly mileage in check to prevent injury.

### Sample Progression Plan

1. Week 1: Long Run 1: 10 miles, Long Run 2: 6 miles
2. Week 2: Long Run 1: 12 miles, Long Run 2: 8 miles
3. Week 3: Long Run 1: 14 miles, Long Run 2: 10 miles
4. Week 4: Recovery Week — reduce overall mileage

## How Do You Manage Tired Legs After Double Runs?

Managing tired legs is vital for recovery and further training. Incorporate strategies like foam rolling, proper hydration, and stretching post-run. Nutrition plays a significant role; prioritize protein intake and anti-inflammatory foods to promote healing.

### Recovery Tips

- Foam roll or use a massage gun to relieve muscle tension.
- Rehydrate with electrolyte-rich beverages.
- Consume protein-rich meals within 30 minutes post-run.

## Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a back to back long run be?
Start with a combined total of around 16 miles over two days and gradually increase based on your training level. Aim for the first run to be longer than the second.

How do back to back long runs help with ultra races?
These runs simulate the exhaustion felt during an ultra, improving endurance and mental toughness. They prepare you for running on tired legs, similar to race conditions.

What are some essential recovery foods post double long runs?
Focus on protein for muscle repair, complex carbohydrates for glycogen replenishment, and foods rich in antioxidants to reduce inflammation.

Should I reduce my daily activities during double long run weeks?
Yes, it's wise to reduce other activities to prevent overtraining. Prioritize rest and recovery to maximize the benefits of your training.

Can beginners do back to back long runs?
Beginners can attempt shorter back to back sessions with ample recovery and should consult training resources to avoid injury. A gradual build-up is essential for safety.

How do I know if I'm overtraining with back to back long runs?
Watch for signs like persistent fatigue, mood changes, or plateauing performance. Schedule rest weeks and adjust your plan if these symptoms arise.
