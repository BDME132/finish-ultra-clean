Chafing is one of the most preventable problems in ultra running and one of the most commonly ignored in training. By the time most runners deal with it seriously, they're limping to a finish line or dropping from a race. Don't be that runner.

## Why Does Chafing Happen on Long Runs?

Chafing happens when skin rubs against skin or fabric repeatedly over time. On a 3-mile run, it doesn't matter. Over 6 hours and 30 miles, that repetition turns minor friction into raw, burning skin. Heat, moisture (sweat), and salt crystals from dried sweat all accelerate the process.

The longer you run, the more important chafing prevention becomes. For any training run over 90 minutes, anti-chafe should be part of your pre-run routine.

## Where Should You Apply Anti-Chafe Before a Long Run?

Apply to every area before every run over 90 minutes. The key spots:

- **Inner thighs** — the most common chafe zone. Apply a thick layer, especially in shorts or tights without a liner.
- **Underarms** — vest shoulder straps cause underarm chafing over long distances. Apply to both the skin and the inside of the shoulder strap.
- **Nipples (men)** — use NipGuard bandages or a thick layer of Body Glide. Shirt friction over 4+ hours is serious. Race photos with blood trails are a rite of passage you can skip.
- **Between toes** — this is better solved with toe socks than balm alone (see below).
- **Back and waist** — where the vest's back panel sits. Apply to any area where the vest contacts bare skin.

## How Does Clothing Choice Prevent Chafing?

Fabric matters. Cotton holds moisture and causes chafing within miles. Synthetic or merino wool fabrics wick moisture and dry quickly.

**For shorts:** Choose lightweight running shorts with a built-in liner. The **[Patagonia Strider Pro](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Patagonia+Strider+Pro+Shorts&tag=finishultra-20)** is the gold standard — the liner eliminates thigh chafing and the fabric dries fast. Avoid loose shorts without a liner on runs over 10 miles.

**For tops:** Avoid seamed cotton shirts. A technical running top with flat seams dramatically reduces shoulder and side chafing under a vest.

**For women:** A well-fitted sports bra with flat seams and anti-chafe construction is essential. Check all contact points with your vest straps.

## What Should You Do If You Start Chafing Mid-Race?

At the first sign of chafing — not when it's already painful, but when you first notice it — stop at the next aid station. Dry the area with a paper towel or bandage, apply fresh anti-chafe generously, and adjust any clothing or gear that's contributing. A 3-minute stop now prevents a 3-hour nightmare later.

Most drop bags should include a travel-size anti-chafe stick, a few bandages, and wipes for a quick clean before reapplying.

## The Pre-Race Chafing Prevention Routine

For races, level up your normal anti-chafe routine:

1. Apply Body Glide or Squirrel's Nut Butter to all 5 key zones
2. Apply Trail Toes to the entire foot and between toes
3. Put on Injinji toe socks, then a second cushion sock on top if the race is long or wet
4. Apply a second pass of balm to inner thighs after getting dressed
5. Pack anti-chafe in your vest pocket and drop bag
