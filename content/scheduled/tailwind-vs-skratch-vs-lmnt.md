Tailwind, Skratch Labs, and LMNT are the three electrolyte products you'll hear about most in ultra running. They serve overlapping but different needs. Here's a direct comparison.

## The Basics: What Each Product Does

**[Tailwind Endurance Fuel](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Tailwind+Endurance+Fuel&tag=finishultra-20) — All-in-one calories + electrolytes**
Tailwind is designed to be your complete fuel source. One or two scoops in water covers both caloric needs and electrolyte replacement. The philosophy: simplify nutrition by putting everything in your bottle.

Per serving (1 scoop, 24oz water): 100 calories, 303mg sodium, 88mg potassium, 17mg magnesium

**[Skratch Labs Sport Hydration](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Skratch+Labs+Sport+Hydration&tag=finishultra-20) — Electrolytes + light calories, real fruit flavor**
Skratch is designed as hydration first, with enough calories to supplement but not replace solid food. Real fruit-based flavoring makes it the best-tasting option at hour 6 when sweetness fatigue hits.

Per serving (1 scoop, 16oz water): 80 calories, 380mg sodium, 39mg potassium, 39mg magnesium

**[LMNT Electrolyte Mix](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LMNT+Electrolyte&tag=finishultra-20) — High sodium, zero sugar**
LMNT is an electrolyte supplement, not a fuel source. It delivers 1,000mg sodium per packet with no sugar. It's meant to be used alongside real food or other caloric products.

Per serving (1 packet, 16oz water): 10 calories, 1,000mg sodium, 200mg potassium, 60mg magnesium

## Which Runner Should Choose Which?

**Choose Tailwind if:** You want the simplest possible nutrition strategy, you've had GI issues with gels or solid food during races, or you're new to ultra running and want to minimize decisions.

**Choose Skratch Labs if:** You prefer eating solid food for calories and want hydration that tastes good without sweetness fatigue, you've found Tailwind too sweet in later race miles, or you're training in moderate conditions.

**Choose LMNT if:** You're a heavy sweater, you cramp in races, you race primarily in heat, or you already have a solid food nutrition strategy and need a sodium supplement.

## The Most Common Combination

Many experienced ultra runners use **Tailwind as the primary fuel in one flask and Skratch Labs or plain water in the other flask** to manage sweetness fatigue. On hot days or for heavy sweaters, they add an LMNT packet or SaltStick capsules on top.

This isn't overcomplicating things — it's addressing different needs with appropriate tools. For beginners, start simple: Tailwind only, both flasks. Add complexity only when you've identified a specific gap in your current strategy.

## The Verdict

For first-time ultra runners: **Tailwind wins** on simplicity, value, and GI tolerance. It's the product most beginner ultra runners should start with.

For experienced runners in heat: **LMNT as a supplement** to Tailwind or Skratch provides the sodium top-up that hard training and hot races demand.

For taste-focused runners late in races: **Skratch Labs** holds up better than Tailwind in terms of palatability at hour 7+.
