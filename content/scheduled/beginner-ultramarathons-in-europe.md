## What Are the Best Beginner Ultramarathons in Europe?

When it comes to beginner ultramarathons in Europe, several races stand out for their stunning scenery and welcoming atmospheres. Unlike intense ultra trails, these selections cater to those who are new to the ultramarathon scene, featuring easier terrains while capturing the essence of trail running. If you're venturing into your first ultra experience, these races are a great starting point.

### Ecotrail Oslo 50K

The Ecotrail Oslo 50K in Norway offers a delightful course that combines urban settings with nature's splendor. Participants run through beautiful forest trails while enjoying views of the Oslofjord, making it a visual treat for anyone tackling a 50-kilometer distance for the first time. With its well-marked paths and moderate elevation changes, it's a popular choice among beginners.

### KKUM 55K

Held in Kielce, Poland, the Kielce Cross Ultramarathon (KKUM) 55K is another beginner-friendly race. The course takes runners through the beautiful Świętokrzyskie Mountains, featuring gentle rolling hills and lush landscapes. This race is celebrated for its friendly atmosphere and community support, making it a comfortable environment for first-timers.

### Dordogne Intégrale

For a more cultural adventure, the Dordogne Intégrale in France is a gem among easy first ultra races in Europe. This ultra takes you through the scenic river valleys of Dordogne, with quaint villages and historic castles lining the route. The relatively flat course and temperate climate offer a forgiving, yet breathtaking, experience.

## What Should You Expect During Your First Ultra?

Completing your first ultramarathon is a profound achievement. However, knowing what to expect can ease pre-race jitters and help you navigate the challenges of the event.

- Longer Duration: Expect to be on your feet for several hours, longer than a regular marathon. 
- Pacing: Remember that ultramarathons are all about pacing. Use a [pace calculator](/tools/pace-calculator) to determine manageable intervals.
- Varied Terrains: Be prepared for a mix of terrains — from forest paths to open fields. Each offers a different kind of challenge.

## How to Train for Your First 50K in Europe?

Training for a 50K ultramarathon is not vastly different from marathon prep, but it does require unique aspects of endurance training and mental fortitude. Detailed planning and structured routines are crucial elements.

### Build a Running Base

Start by building a solid running base with consistent weekly mileage. Mixing in long runs on weekends is essential to building the stamina required for an ultra. Aim for back-to-back long runs as you advance in your training regime.

### Incorporate Trail Runs

Get comfortable running on trails, as road training does not directly translate to trail performance. Check out our guide on [beginner trail races](/training/first-50k) to understand how terrain impacts your approach and energy conservation.
