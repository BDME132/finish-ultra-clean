The long runs in your training plan are also the best time to consume ultra running content. These podcasts will keep you company through your 20-milers and teach you things that take other runners years to figure out.

## 1. Trail Runner Nation

**Best for:** Broad coverage, beginner accessibility, training science

Trail Runner Nation has been running since 2010 and has one of the largest back catalogs in trail running. Episodes cover everything from first-timer advice to elite race strategy. The hosts are experienced runners who interview coaches, researchers, and runners at all levels. Start with their beginner series or search for episodes on topics relevant to your current training phase.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, trailrunnernation.com

## 2. Some Work All Play (SWAP)

**Best for:** Training science, coaching perspective, humor

David Roche is a coach and professional runner who covers training science in an accessible, occasionally ridiculous format with his co-host Megan Roche (also a doctor). This podcast cuts through running mythology with evidence-based content and genuinely funny presentation. Essential listening for understanding why training plans work the way they do.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts

## 3. Ultrarunner Podcast

**Best for:** Long-form race reports, community depth

Deep-dive interviews with ultra runners across all levels — from first-time 50K finishers to Western States veterans. The race reports from runners who just finished major events are particularly useful for understanding what races actually feel like from the inside.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, ultrarunnercast.com

## 4. Dirt Church Radio

**Best for:** Community, culture, humor, accessibility

Dirt Church covers trail running culture with less focus on elite performance and more on the experience of being a runner in the world. Funny, honest, and unpretentious — refreshing compared to the achievement-focused tone of some running media.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts

## 5. The Morning Shakeout

**Best for:** Broad running culture, thoughtful long-form interviews

Mario Fraioli hosts thoughtful long-form interviews with coaches, runners, and figures from across the running world. Not exclusively ultra focused but consistently high quality. Good for the longer long runs when you want substance rather than entertainment.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, themorningshakeout.com

## 6. Run to the Top (Runners Connect)

**Best for:** Training science and injury prevention

Runners Connect's podcast covers running science with a focus on practical training applications. Episodes on injury prevention, nutrition, and training theory are particularly useful for runners building their first serious training block.

**Find it:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, runnersconnect.net

## What to Listen to During Long Runs

The research on podcast listening during long runs suggests it increases time-to-fatigue and makes long efforts feel shorter — particularly valuable for treadmill training where mental fatigue accumulates fast.

A practical approach: reserve podcasts for your longest runs (the ones where you need the most mental support). Run your shorter easy runs without audio to practice the focus you'll need on race day without music or podcasts.

## Video: The Best Ultra Running Films to Watch

If podcasts aren't enough, several ultra running documentaries provide the emotional connection to the sport that audio cannot. *Desert Runners* (Amazon Prime) follows four non-elite runners through the 4 Deserts race series. *The Long Run* covers the Western States 100. Billy Yang's YouTube channel has dozens of beautifully made short films about ultra running culture.

These are what to watch the week before your race when you need a reminder of why you're doing this.
