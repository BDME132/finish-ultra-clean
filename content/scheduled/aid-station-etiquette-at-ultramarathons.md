Quick Answer: Aid station etiquette at an ultramarathon revolves around efficient, respectful interactions with volunteers and fellow runners. Prioritize planning ahead for stops, communicating clearly, disposing of trash in designated areas, and expressing gratitude to those assisting you. Practicing good manners ensures a smoother race experience for everyone involved.

## What Should You Expect at an Aid Station in an Ultramarathon?

Approaching an aid station in your first ultramarathon can seem intimidating, but being prepared helps tremendously. Expect to find tables laden with drinks, snacks, and specific supplies, staffed by volunteers eager to assist you. Aid stations often have a combination of water, electrolyte drinks, fruits, salty snacks, and gels — each designed to meet your nutritional needs during the race.

### How Can You Plan Ahead to Use Aid Stations Effectively?

Preparation is key to making your aid station stops efficient. Familiarize yourself with the aid station locations and what they offer before race day. Having a plan means knowing exactly what you need, whether it's refueling with electrolytes, replenishing your gels, or grabbing a quick snack. Using a [pace calculator](/tools/pace-calculator) can help you time your arrival at each station.

## How Should You Interact with Volunteers?

Volunteers are the backbone of ultramarathons, offering invaluable support. When approaching an aid station, make eye contact, smile, and if possible, call out your needs in advance. Clearly communicating what you need, whether it’s "water please," or "salt tablets," allows them to assist you efficiently.

### Why is Gratitude Essential?

Expressing gratitude to aid station volunteers is not only polite but can boost morale for both parties. A simple "thank you" goes a long way. Showing appreciation makes their day more rewarding and enhances the overall positive atmosphere of the race.

## How Do You Dispose of Trash Properly at Aid Stations?

Keeping the race environment clean is a responsibility shared by all ultramarathon participants. Always dispose of trash in designated bins. If bins are crowded, carry your trash to the next station or until you find an appropriate place to discard it. Respecting the environment reflects good [aid station etiquette in an ultramarathon](/training/first-50k).

### What If You Miss the Trash Bin?

If you accidentally miss a bin, take the time to pick it up and find the right spot. We've all made mistakes under pressure, but fixing them is part of showing respect to your fellow runners and the environment.

## What Are the Best Practices for Efficient Aid Station Stops?

Efficient aid station behavior is about speed and consideration. Move to the side after getting what you need, allowing others their turn. Organize your gear before arriving at a station so transitions are quick and smooth. Consider wearing a running pack like the [Salomon Advanced Skin 12](/gear) to keep essentials easily accessible.

### How Do You Navigate Crowded Stations?

In a crowded aid station, patience and courtesy are crucial. If lines are forming, wait your turn and encourage faster runners to pass through. Keeping pathways clear ensures everyone can get in and out efficiently, maintaining a steady flow.

## Frequently Asked Questions

What should I carry in my pack to minimize aid station trips?  
Carry essentials like gels, salt tablets, and a hydration system. Consider packs like the [Nathan Pinnacle 4L on Amazon](https://amzn.to/4bObB06), which offer storage for snacks and fluids, letting you breeze through stations without frequent stops.

How do I train for smooth aid station transitions?  
Practicing during long runs by simulating aid stops with your team or alone helps you develop a system. Refining what you need and how you interact comes with experience and practice.

Should I avoid trying new food or drinks at aid stations?  
Yes, stick to what your stomach knows. New foods might cause digestive issues during a race. Research what will be provided beforehand, and try these during training to avoid surprises.

How to thank aid station volunteers effectively?  
Acknowledge volunteers with a smile and thank them genuinely. A few words of appreciation can uplift their spirits and create a friendly atmosphere.

Can I bring my support team to aid stations?  
Most ultramarathons allow crew support but confirm specific race rules beforehand. Your crew should be respectful of the space and help you quickly without obstructing other runners.

What are some common mistakes to avoid at aid stations?  
Avoid lingering too long without reason, blocking pathways, and failing to communicate your needs. Be swift, considerate, and always keep moving to help maintain an efficient aid station flow.
