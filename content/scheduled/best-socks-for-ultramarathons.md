## How do I Prevent Blisters During an Ultramarathon?

Preventing blisters is crucial for a successful ultramarathon experience. The key is choosing the right socks, combined with a few other strategic measures. Start with socks designed for blister prevention, like Injinji or Darn Tough. Ensure your shoes fit properly to avoid unnecessary movement, which can lead to friction. Keep your feet dry by changing socks if they get wet and consider using a blister prevention balm on potential hot spots before you start running.

### Tips for Blister Prevention

- Shoe Fit: Ensure a snug fit that allows for swelling but avoids excessive movement.
- Sock Material: Opt for moisture-wicking materials to keep feet dry.
- Layering: Some runners prefer wearing a thin liner sock beneath a thicker sock for added protection.
- Foot Care: Regularly check your feet for hot spots and address them immediately.

## Why Is Moisture Management Important for Trail Running Socks?

Moisture management is critical in any running sock, especially for ultramarathons. Wet feet can quickly lead to blisters, and prolonged moisture can also result in fungal infections. Good trail running socks, like those from Injinji and Darn Tough, use high-quality materials designed to pull moisture away from your skin and keep your feet as dry as possible, even during wet trail conditions.

### Best Materials for Moisture Management

- Merino Wool: Naturally wicks moisture and maintains insulating properties when wet.
- Synthetic Blends: Often include polyester or nylon, which also provide excellent moisture-wicking and quick-drying qualities.

## How Often Should You Change Socks During an Ultramarathon?

Changing socks during an ultramarathon can be a game-changer in terms of comfort and blister prevention. Ideally, you should plan to change your socks at significant checkpoints, especially if you're running more than 50 miles. Fresh socks help in removing debris that might have built up and ensure continued moisture management as you run.

Are merino wool socks good for ultramarathons?

Choose socks that fit snugly without being too tight. Refer to each brand’s size chart as variations may exist. Ensure there’s no slippage or excessive tightness.
