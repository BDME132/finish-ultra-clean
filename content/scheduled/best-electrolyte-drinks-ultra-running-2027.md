Electrolyte products multiply every year. Most are not designed for ultra running — they're designed for gym workouts or casual use and fall short on sodium. Here's what actually works for 6+ hours of sustained effort.

## What Ultra Runners Actually Need From Electrolytes

The primary electrolyte need for ultra runners is **sodium**: 500–1,000mg per hour depending on sweat rate and temperature. Secondary needs are potassium, magnesium, and calcium. Most mainstream sports drinks provide 100–200mg sodium per serving — far below what multi-hour ultra running demands.

The product you choose needs to deliver real sodium replacement, not just electrolyte marketing language.

## 1. Tailwind Endurance Fuel — Best All-in-One

**Sodium per serving:** 303mg | **Calories:** 100 | **Price:** ~$25 for 30 servings

[Tailwind](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Tailwind+Endurance+Fuel&tag=finishultra-20) is the most recommended beginner ultra running nutrition product for good reason: it delivers calories and electrolytes in one clean formula with no clumping, no GI issues, and flavors that stay tolerable over many hours. Mix one scoop in 24oz water.

**Best for:** First-time ultra runners, runners who want simplest possible nutrition, anyone who has had GI issues with gels
**Limitation:** Sodium is moderate — heavy sweaters may need supplementation on hot days

## 2. LMNT — Best for Heavy Sweaters and Heat

**Sodium per serving:** 1,000mg | **Calories:** 10 | **Price:** ~$45 for 30 servings

[LMNT](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LMNT+Electrolyte&tag=finishultra-20) delivers the highest sodium of any mainstream electrolyte product at 1,000mg per packet. No sugar, no filler — just sodium, potassium, and magnesium. The salty taste is an acquired preference, but runners who sweat heavily and cramp frequently often credit LMNT with ending their cramping problems.

**Best for:** Heavy sweaters, hot-weather racing, runners who cramp
**Limitation:** No calories — must eat separately. Expensive per serving.

## 3. SaltStick Caps — Best Supplemental Capsule

**Sodium per capsule:** 215mg | **Calories:** 0 | **Price:** ~$22 for 100 capsules

[SaltStick](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=SaltStick+Caps&tag=finishultra-20) capsules are the most popular supplemental electrolyte among experienced ultra runners. Swallow 1–2 per hour with water in addition to your primary nutrition. The capsule format is convenient for runners who want to separate their electrolyte intake from their caloric intake.

**Best for:** Supplementing any liquid fuel strategy, hot-weather racing, runners who prefer precise control
**Limitation:** Requires remembering to take capsules — some runners prefer the passive intake of mixed drinks

## 4. Skratch Labs Sport Hydration — Best Tasting

**Sodium per serving:** 380mg | **Calories:** 80 | **Price:** ~$22 for 20 servings

[Skratch Labs](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Skratch+Labs+Sport+Hydration&tag=finishultra-20) uses real fruit for flavoring and a clean formula that is consistently praised for taste, particularly over long hours when sweeter products become unpleasant. Sodium is slightly higher than Tailwind. Fewer caloric density means you'll need to eat more solid food.

**Best for:** Runners who find Tailwind too sweet at hour 6+, mixed with solid food nutrition
**Limitation:** Fewer calories per serving means more solid food required

## 5. Maurten Drink Mix — Best for Race-Day Intensity

**Sodium per serving:** 500mg | **Calories:** 160 | **Price:** ~$50 for 14 servings

[Maurten](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Maurten+Drink+Mix&tag=finishultra-20) uses a hydrogel technology that allows higher carbohydrate concentration without the stomach issues typical of high-sugar drinks. At nearly $4 per serving, it's expensive for training use — but for runners doing competitive 50Ks who want maximum carbohydrate delivery with minimal GI risk, it's worth considering for race day.

**Best for:** Competitive runners, race-day-only use
**Limitation:** Price makes it impractical for training. Neutral taste is polarizing.

## The Bottom Line

Start with Tailwind if you're new to ultra nutrition — it covers calories and electrolytes in one simple product. Add LMNT or SaltStick if you race in heat or have cramping issues. Upgrade to Maurten or Skratch if you want to optimize further once you've found your nutritional baseline.

Never try a new electrolyte product on race day. Test everything on long training runs first.
