## Why Use Trekking Poles in an Ultramarathon?

Trekking poles are a game-changer in ultramarathons, especially for beginners navigating tough terrains. They help distribute your weight and reduce impact on your knees and joints during long descents. This added stability makes them particularly useful in uneven terrain or when fatigue sets in. By engaging your upper body, they also allow your legs to get a slight reprieve, delaying the onset of fatigue.

### What Are the Best Trekking Pole Features for Beginners?

When looking for trekking poles, prioritize weight and adjustability. Lightweight carbon fiber poles like the [Black Diamond Distance 8 on Amazon](#) offer an excellent balance for those new to the sport. Adjustability is crucial as it allows you to tailor the length to the task, such as shortening for uphill climbs and lengthening for downhill or flat stretches. Comfortable grips and durable tips are also considerations that can impact your overall experience.

## When Should You Use Trekking Poles During an Ultramarathon?

Using trekking poles strategically throughout your race can be a godsend. They are most beneficial during uphill sections, rough terrain, or when your legs are exceedingly fatigued. Practice using them in these scenarios during training so it feels second nature during the race. On flat or downhill segments, you might prefer to stow them away in your running pack to maximize speed and conserve arm energy.

## What Technique Should Beginners Use with Trekking Poles?

Mastering trekking pole technique is crucial to making them work for you rather than against you. Aim to synchronize your stride with arm movement: as your right foot steps forward, your left pole should plant slightly ahead. This cross-pattern mirrors your natural walking rhythm and maximizes efficiency. Use your poles to push off during uphill segments and for balance on technical descents.

### Practicing Uphill Techniques

For uphills, plant the poles slightly ahead and pull your body upwards by firmly pushing off with your arms. This not only assists your leg muscles but also actively engages your upper body, distributing the workload more evenly. Simulate race conditions by practicing on steep trails.

## How to Introduce Trekking Poles in Training

Start incorporating poles into your regular training to become comfortable with their use. Training with poles might feel awkward initially, especially if you're transitioning from shorter distances, but persistence pays off. Begin with shorter hikes or trails and slowly integrate them into your long runs. Monitor how they assist your performance and identify areas for technique improvement. 

### Assessing Your Progress

Regularly assess your comfort level and efficiency with poles during training sessions. If you find any discomfort in your wrists or arms, adjust your technique or pole settings. Regular use should feel natural and advantageous, not burdensome.

## How to Choose the Right Trekking Poles for You

Choosing the right trekking poles for an ultramarathon depends on several individual preferences and needs. Focus on:

- Material: Lighter materials like carbon fiber reduce fatigue over long distances but might be more expensive.
- Collapsed Length: Consider collapsible poles for easy pack storage during non-use sections.
- Grip Comfort: Cork grips provide a comfortable, sweat-wicking hold during prolonged use.
- Shock Absorption: Built-in shock absorbers can add comfort on rocky, hard-packed trails.

Assessing these features against your specific race and training goals will ensure you have the optimal setup for your ultramarathon journey. Explore different models and find what feels right for your style and needs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are trekking poles worth the investment for beginners?

Yes, especially if you'll be tackling technical, hilly courses in your ultramarathon. They can help maintain stability, conserve energy, and reduce impact on joints, making them worth the investment.

### Should I train with trekking poles before my race?

Absolutely, training with trekking poles will help you get familiar with their usage, improving your technique and comfort during the actual race. It's essential to integrate them into your routine to make their application second nature.

### How do I know if the poles I have are the right ones for me?

The right poles should feel comfortable, easy to adjust, and suit the terrain of your race. They should help you, not hinder you, and fit well in your running gear setup.

### What is the proper height adjustment for trekking poles?

A good starting point is to adjust the pole so your elbows form a roughly 90-degree angle when holding the grip. Test this setting during training and adjust as necessary to suit different terrains.

### How do I prevent my hands from getting sore while using trekking poles?

Ensure you're using proper grip technique; avoid gripping too tightly. Let your body weight and the poles work together rather than relying solely on your arm strength. Comfortable, padded straps can also alleviate some of this strain.

### Can I use Nordic walking poles for ultramarathons?

While similar, Nordic walking poles are generally not designed for rugged terrain and ultramarathon demands. Opt for specialized trekking poles built for longer endurance events and rough trails for better performance and durability.
