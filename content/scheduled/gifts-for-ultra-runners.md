Ultra runners are hard to buy for if you don't know what you're doing. The gear is technical, sizing matters, and experienced runners tend to have strong opinions. These gifts work because they're either consumables (always needed) or universally useful.

## Under $20: The Always-Appreciated Consumables

**[Injinji Trail Midweight Crew Socks](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Injinji+Trail+Midweight+Crew&tag=finishultra-20) — $18**
The toe socks that prevent between-the-toe blisters. Ultra runners go through socks constantly. A fresh pair is always welcome, and Injinji is the brand most trail runners already trust.

**[Body Glide Original Anti-Chafe Balm](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Body+Glide+Original&tag=finishultra-20) — $9–$12**
The anti-chafe product that every ultra runner uses on every long run. It goes fast. A fresh stick is practical, not glamorous — and practical is what ultra runners actually want.

**[Squirrel's Nut Butter](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Squirrels+Nut+Butter&tag=finishultra-20) — $14**
The cult-favorite alternative to Body Glide, preferred by many runners for ultras over 8 hours. A small tin fits in any vest pocket. If the runner you're buying for does long races, they'll love this.

**[Spring Energy Awesome Sauce Gels](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Spring+Energy+Awesome+Sauce&tag=finishultra-20) — $4–$5 each, pack of 6 ~$25**
Real-food gels made from rice and fruit. A 6-pack covers several long training runs and is a genuinely useful training supply.

## $20–$35: The Practical Upgrades

**[Tailwind Endurance Fuel (30-serving bag)](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Tailwind+Endurance+Fuel&tag=finishultra-20) — $25**
The all-in-one nutrition that thousands of ultra runners use as their primary fuel. One bag covers 8–10 long runs. If they don't already use it, this is the ideal introduction. If they do use it, you just saved them a resupply trip.

**[LMNT Electrolyte Variety Pack](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LMNT+Electrolyte+Variety+Pack&tag=finishultra-20) — $25–$30**
High-sodium electrolyte mix in 8 flavors. Popular with runners who run in heat or sweat heavily. The variety pack lets them find a favorite flavor.

**[SaltStick Electrolyte Capsules](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=SaltStick+Caps&tag=finishultra-20) — $22**
100-capsule bottle of electrolyte tabs. Practical for race-day supplementation and hot-weather training. Every ultra runner eventually goes through a bottle of these.

**[Trail Toes Ultra-Distance Foot Cream](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Trail+Toes+Ultra&tag=finishultra-20) — $20**
Petroleum-based foot protectant specifically designed for long trail runs. Reduces blisters, skin breakdown, and hot spots. A product many runners have heard of but haven't tried.

## $35–$50: The Slightly More Special Gifts

**[Black Diamond Spot 400 Headlamp](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Black+Diamond+Spot+400&tag=finishultra-20) — $40**
400-lumen headlamp — enough for fast night running on technical trails. Waterproof, rechargeable, and compact. Many runners own older headlamps and haven't upgraded. This is a meaningful practical upgrade.

**[Petzl Actik Core Headlamp](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Petzl+Actik+Core&tag=finishultra-20) — $60 (slightly over budget, worth mentioning)**
The most popular entry-level headlamp in ultra running. 350 lumens, rechargeable via USB, lightweight. If you can stretch slightly over $50, this is one of the most universally useful gifts on this list.

**REI Gift Card — $35–$50**
Unglamorous but always right. Ultra runners have specific gear needs and sizes that are hard to guess. A gift card to REI, Amazon, or UltraSignup (for race entries) lets them get exactly what they need.

## What Not to Buy

Avoid running shoes (sizing and fit are too personal), running vests (same reason), GPS watches (the runner likely has preferences), and novelty running gifts (fun the first time, rarely used). When in doubt, consumables and headlamps are the safest bets.
