Quick Answer: To qualify for the Western States 100, you must complete one of the official qualifying races within the allocated time during the qualifying period. Each year, runners need to enter the Western States lottery after finishing a designated race, which then gives them a chance to secure a spot in the prestigious ultramarathon.

## What Are the Steps to Qualify for the Western States 100?

Qualifying for the Western States 100 is a multi-step process involving both preparation and a bit of luck. First, you need to complete one of the official qualifying races within the specified time. These races vary from 50K to 100 miles in length and can be found on the Western States website. Next, you must enter the lottery system. Purchasing a lottery ticket automatically enters you into the draw for a spot in the race.

### What Are Western States Qualifying Races?

A qualifying race is required to be eligible for the Western States lottery. Each year, Western States revises the list of qualifying races, which typically includes well-known events like the Lake Sonoma 50, the Javelina Jundred 100K, and the Bandera 100K. Completing any one of these races under the designated time garners you an entry into the lottery.

For a complete list of qualifying races, and to explore your options, make sure you check the official race website or visit our [training page](/training/first-50k).

## How Does the Western States Lottery Work?

The Western States lottery is the primary avenue for securing a spot in the race and is driven by a fair yet competitive system. Each year, runners who have completed a qualifying race can purchase a lottery ticket. The lottery itself is held in December for the race that takes place the following June.

### Tips for Maximizing Your Lottery Chances

1. Run Consecutive Years: Each year you complete a qualifying race but don't secure a spot in the lottery, you gain an additional ticket for the next year. This cumulative process enhances your odds over time.
2. Target Golden Ticket Races: Winning one of the designated "Golden Ticket" races, like the Canyons Endurance 100K or the Run Rabbit Run 100-mile race, guarantees you a slot in the Western States. Training for and attempting these races might increase your chances dramatically when compared to relying solely on luck.

## What's Racing in the Western States 100 Like?

Running the Western States 100 mile race is both exhilarating and grueling, demanding not just physical stamina but also mental fortitude. Starting in Olympic Valley, California, and finishing in Auburn, this race crosses through the stunning yet challenging Sierra Nevada mountains.

## How Should You Train to Qualify for Western States 100?

Training for a qualifying race and, ultimately, Western States 100 itself is a substantial endeavor that involves a blend of long runs, hill workouts, and back-to-back training days. Here is a basic framework:

- Base Building: Spend at least a few months building endurance through successive long runs to strengthen your legs and aerobic capacity.
- Elevation Training: Given the mountainous terrains of most qualifying races, incorporate regular hill workouts.
- Back-to-Back Long Runs: This technique trains your body to handle fatigue and mimic race conditions.
- Rest and Recovery: Prioritize adequate rest to allow your body to adapt to the increasing demands of training.

Chances vary based on the number of tickets you hold. First-time entrants have lower odds compared to those who've accumulated multiple years of qualifiers due to the weighted lottery system.

A Golden Ticket race is a specific event where top finishers receive automatic entry into the Western States. Winning a Golden Ticket event bypasses the lottery necessity.
