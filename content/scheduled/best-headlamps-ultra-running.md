Your headlamp is safety gear, not just convenience gear. A headlamp failure in a remote night section is a genuine emergency. A dim headlamp on technical downhill terrain causes falls. Choosing right matters.

## What Actually Matters When Choosing a Running Headlamp

**Lumens (brightness):** The only spec that matters most. For trail running, minimum 200 lumens. 350–500 is the comfortable range for most terrain. 700+ is for fast technical racing.

**Beam pattern:** A mixed beam with central spot and peripheral flood is best for running — the spot illuminates the trail ahead while the flood provides peripheral depth perception for footfalls. Pure spot beams cause tunnel vision; pure flood beams lack distance.

**Weight:** Running-specific headlamps should weigh 60–120g. Standard hiking headlamps at 200g+ create noticeable head movement while running and cause neck fatigue over hours.

**Battery life:** Real-world battery life at a usable brightness level (not maximum) should be 4–8 hours minimum. Verify this at the brightness level you'll actually use.

**Rechargeability:** USB rechargeable headlamps are strongly preferred — you can top them off from any power bank. AAA battery lamps require you to carry spare batteries.

**Stability on head:** The headlamp should not move or bounce while running. Test this at a run in the store if possible. The strap and rear balance point matter significantly.

## The Essential Backup Light

Every runner using a headlamp for 2+ hours of night running should carry a backup. The [Coast G20 Inspection Light](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Coast+G20+flashlight&tag=finishultra-20) ($15) clips to a vest strap or fits in a pocket and weighs 40g. It won't replace your headlamp but will get you to an aid station if your primary fails.

Most races with mandatory gear lists require a backup light source. Check before race day.

## Headlamp Race Day Checklist

- Headlamp fully charged (or fresh batteries) the night before
- Backup light in vest pocket
- Test both lights work before leaving for race start
- Know the on/off/mode buttons by feel in the dark (test this at home with eyes closed)
- If rechargeable, bring the charging cable to the race start just in case
