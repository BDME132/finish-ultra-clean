Your first 50K should be fun, not a survival test. The races on this list were selected specifically for beginners: forgiving terrain, generous time limits, excellent volunteer support, and communities that cheer every runner home.

Race dates and registration windows change year to year — verify all details at the race website before registering.

## What Makes a 50K Beginner-Friendly?

A beginner-friendly 50K has four characteristics: **under 3,500 feet of total elevation gain**, a **cutoff time of at least 10 hours**, **aid stations every 4–6 miles**, and a **reputation for welcoming first-timers**. All 10 races on this list meet those criteria.

## 1. Caumsett 50K — Lloyd Neck, New York (March)

Caumsett is as flat as trail racing gets — run entirely through Lloyd Neck State Park on carriage roads and smooth trails. The 6-loop format means you pass the start/finish area repeatedly, which is psychologically helpful for first-timers. Cutoff is generous. Community is welcoming. If you're in the Northeast, this is the ideal first race.

**Elevation:** ~500 feet | **Format:** 6 loops | **Typical cutoff:** 9 hours
**Website:** caumsett50k.com

## 2. Ice Age Trail 50K — La Grange, Wisconsin (May)

The Ice Age Trail 50K runs on the eponymous trail through kettle moraines and glacial terrain in Wisconsin. It's a UTMB-qualifying race with a well-earned reputation for organization, volunteer support, and runner-friendly logistics. The trail is rolling but not technical — runnable throughout for a well-trained beginner.

**Elevation:** ~2,600 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 9 hours
**Website:** iceagetrail.org

## 3. Pineland Farms Trail Running Festival 50K — New Gloucester, Maine (May)

Pineland is one of the best-organized trail events in the Northeast. The 50K runs laps around a working farm on mostly flat, groomed trails. The festival atmosphere — multiple distances running simultaneously, food vendors, spectators — makes it ideal for nervous first-timers.

**Elevation:** ~1,800 feet | **Format:** Loops | **Typical cutoff:** 10 hours
**Website:** pinelandfarms.org/trail-running

## 4. Holiday Lake 50K++ — Appomattox, Virginia (February)

Holiday Lake runs through the Appalachian foothills on flat forest trails around a state park. The "++" in the name means the course is actually about 52 miles — but the terrain and cutoff are forgiving enough that it remains a popular first ultra. Cold and muddy in February, which is good mental training.

**Elevation:** ~2,500 feet | **Format:** Out-and-back loops | **Typical cutoff:** 11 hours
**Website:** friendsoftheholidaylake50k.com

## 5. Gorge Waterfalls 50K — Hood River, Oregon (April)

One of the most scenic 50Ks in the country, running along the Columbia River Gorge past multiple waterfalls. The course is not technical but has meaningful elevation. The trail quality, aid station food, and scenery make it worth the slightly harder effort. Popular enough that it typically sells out fast — register early.

**Elevation:** ~4,500 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 10 hours
**Website:** gorgewaterfalls.com

## 6. Burning River 50K — Cuyahoga Valley, Ohio (July)

Burning River runs the same trails as the legendary Burning River 100, which means fantastic trail conditions and an experienced volunteer crew. The 50K version is a great entry point — you experience the full ultra atmosphere without the overnight section. Ohio summer heat is your main challenge.

**Elevation:** ~2,800 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 10 hours
**Website:** arrunning.com

## 7. Twisted Branch 100K / 50K — Penn Yan, New York (August)

The Twisted Branch 50K runs through Finger Lakes wine country on the Finger Lakes Trail. Moderate terrain, beautiful scenery, and a culture that celebrates every finisher. Well-supplied aid stations and a strong community of experienced runners who remember their first races.

**Elevation:** ~3,200 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 11 hours
**Website:** twistedbranch.com

## 8. Pinhoti 100 50K — Sylacauga, Alabama (November)

The Pinhoti 50K shares trails with one of the South's most respected 100-milers. The terrain is moderate, the fall temperatures are ideal, and the race culture is deeply welcoming to first-timers. Running in November means you can train through summer and arrive in peak fitness.

**Elevation:** ~3,000 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 10 hours
**Website:** pinhoti100.com

## 9. Arrowhead Ultra 50K — Grand Marais, Minnesota (January)

If you want a story to tell, the Arrowhead 50K runs through the Boundary Waters region in January. Cold, snowy, and surreal — this is not your typical beginner race, but the community is extraordinarily supportive and the experience is unforgettable. Only consider this one if you have cold-weather trail running experience.

**Elevation:** ~1,200 feet | **Format:** Point-to-point | **Typical cutoff:** 16 hours
**Website:** arrowheadultra.com

## 10. Quadrock 25K/50K — Fort Collins, Colorado (May)

Quadrock is accessible from Denver and Fort Collins, runs through beautiful Rocky Mountain foothills, and offers a well-organized race with strong beginner support. The 50K has real elevation but is manageable for a trained beginner. One of the best entry points for mountain ultra running in the West.

**Elevation:** ~5,500 feet | **Format:** Loop | **Typical cutoff:** 12 hours
**Website:** quadrocktrailrace.com
